Pranav Harikumar

Most of my work is aimed around building artificial intelligence in a way that represents the human brain. This has appeared in my work through research on EBMs, Hopfield networks, and neuromorphic hardware.

Most recently, I have been exploring neuromorphic hardware through memristors. By keeping memory, representation, and computation closer to the hardware substrate rather than repeatedly translating everything through binary abstractions, I believe we can improve energy efficiency, latency, adaptability, and eventually richer forms of world modelling. I am also exploring adjacent concepts like SNNs and Dynamic Vision Sensors.

Previously, I worked on software research: code pipelines that achieved better performance than GPT-4.1 while using GPT-4.1, and governed memory pipelines for gated append-only systems, multi-agent orchestration, universal memory, and more. Paper coming out soon hopefully.
