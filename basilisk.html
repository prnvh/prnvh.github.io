The Basilisk Is the Race, Not the Model

AI risk is often framed as a story about a future machine becoming hostile. I think the more immediate danger is the race around it.

The modern basilisk is not a single model waiting somewhere in the future. It is the incentive structure that makes companies, states, workers, researchers, and users feel forced to accelerate.

That matters because the realistic safety frontier is not only model capability. It is the architecture around models: permissions, memory, verification, sandboxing, deployment rules, evaluation, liability, and control.

Trying to personalize the problem into one company or one person misses the system. The race continues as long as the incentives continue. The real question is whether we build enough structure around the systems we are already making.
